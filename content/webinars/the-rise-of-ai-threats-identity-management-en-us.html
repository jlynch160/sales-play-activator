The Rise of AI Threats in Identity Management

MajorKey Technologies Webinar AI Threats Deepfakes Identity Verification

Summary

Examines how artificial intelligence is being weaponized to attack identity systems - from deepfake voice and video used in social engineering to AI-generated phishing and automated credential stuffing. Also covers how AI can be used defensively for identity threat detection.

Key Takeaways

  • AI-powered deepfakes can now convincingly impersonate executives in video calls and voice messages, enabling sophisticated social engineering attacks
  • Automated credential stuffing powered by AI can test millions of username/password combinations with human-like patterns to avoid detection
  • AI-generated phishing emails are grammatically perfect and contextually relevant, making them significantly harder for users to identify
  • Defensive AI in identity security uses behavioral analytics to detect anomalous access patterns that indicate compromised accounts
  • Organizations need to update their identity verification processes to account for AI threats - knowledge-based authentication is no longer sufficient
