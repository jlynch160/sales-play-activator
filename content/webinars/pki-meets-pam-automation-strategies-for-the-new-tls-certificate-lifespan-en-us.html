PKI Meets PAM - Automation for TLS Certificate Lifespan

MajorKey Technologies Webinar PKI Certificate Automation Machine Identity

Summary

Explores the intersection of PKI certificate management and privileged access management as TLS certificate lifespans shorten to 47 days. Covers why manual certificate management is unsustainable, how PAM and PKI automation work together, and strategies for building a unified machine identity program.

Key Takeaways

  • TLS certificate lifespans are shrinking from one year to 47 days, making manual renewal impossible at enterprise scale
  • PAM secures who has access, PKI secures what is trusted - together they form the foundation of machine identity management
  • Automation for certificate renewal must be governed with the same rigor as privileged human access to prevent unauthorized changes
  • Organizations managing hundreds or thousands of certificates need centralized visibility and automated lifecycle management to prevent outages
  • The convergence of PAM and PKI creates an opportunity to build a unified machine identity program rather than managing them as separate silos
